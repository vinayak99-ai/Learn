## 1. Introduction to Plume Network

It serves as the first full-stack blockchain infrastructure that enables seamless tokenization, management, and trading of real-world assets on-chain. Launched in mid-2025, Plume bridges the gap between traditional finance (TradFi) and decentralized finance (DeFi) by providing a compliant, secure, and scalable platform for bringing real-world assets onto the blockchain.

## 2. Core Concepts

### Real World Asset Finance (RWAfi)

**RWAfi** is the practice of tokenizing real-world assets and integrating them into decentralized finance protocols.

**What is RWA Tokenization?**
- The process of converting ownership rights of physical or traditional financial assets into digital tokens on a blockchain
- Each token represents a fractional or whole ownership stake in the underlying asset
- Tokens can be traded, used as collateral, or integrated into DeFi protocols

**Benefits of RWA Tokenization:**
- **Liquidity:** Previously illiquid assets (like real estate) can be traded 24/7
- **Fractional Ownership:** High-value assets can be divided into smaller, affordable pieces
- **Transparency:** All transactions are recorded on-chain with immutable records
- **Accessibility:** Global access to investment opportunities regardless of location
- **Efficiency:** Reduced intermediaries and faster settlement times

### EVM Compatibility

**Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) Compatibility** means Plume can execute smart contracts written for Ethereum.

**Benefits:**
- **Developer Familiarity:** Developers can use familiar tools like Solidity, Hardhat, and Truffle
- **Easy Migration:** Existing Ethereum dApps can be deployed on Plume with minimal modifications
- **Tooling Ecosystem:** Access to the extensive Ethereum development ecosystem
- **Interoperability:** Seamless integration with other EVM-compatible chains
- **Security:** Leverage battle-tested Ethereum security practices and auditing tools

### Modular Architecture

Plume utilizes a **modular L1/L2 design** that separates different blockchain functions into specialized layers.

**Architecture Components:**

| Layer | Function | Benefits |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **Execution Layer** | Processes transactions and smart contracts | High throughput and customizable gas fees |
| **Settlement Layer** | Finalizes transactions and maintains security | Enhanced security and finality guarantees |
| **Data Availability Layer** | Stores and distributes transaction data | Efficient data management and accessibility |
| **Consensus Layer** | Validates transactions and maintains network agreement | Scalable and energy-efficient consensus |

**Advantages of Modular Design:**
- **Scalability:** Each layer can be optimized independently for performance
- **Flexibility:** Easier to upgrade individual components without affecting the entire system
- **Cost Efficiency:** Optimized resource allocation reduces transaction costs
- **Specialization:** Each layer can use the best technology for its specific purpose

## 12. Glossary

### A-E

**AML (Anti-Money Laundering):** Regulations and procedures designed to prevent the use of financial systems for money laundering activities.

**Blockchain:** A distributed ledger technology that records transactions across multiple computers in a secure and immutable way.

**Bridge:** A protocol that enables the transfer of assets or data between different blockchain networks.

**Collateral:** Assets pledged to secure a loan, which can be seized if the borrower defaults.

**Cross-Chain:** Refers to technology or protocols that enable interaction between different blockchain networks.

**Custody:** The safekeeping and management of digital or physical assets on behalf of clients.

**DeFi (Decentralized Finance):** Financial services and applications built on blockchain technology without traditional intermediaries.

**EVM (Ethereum Virtual Machine):** The runtime environment for executing smart contracts on Ethereum and compatible blockchains.

### F-L

**Fractional Ownership:** Dividing ownership of an asset into smaller, more affordable portions that can be owned by multiple parties.

**Gas Fees:** Transaction costs paid to process operations on a blockchain network.

**Governance:** The process by which token holders participate in decision-making for a blockchain protocol or DAO.

**KYC (Know Your Customer):** Identity verification procedures required by financial institutions to verify the identity of their clients.

**L1 (Layer 1):** The base blockchain layer that has its own native token and consensus mechanism (e.g., Ethereum, Bitcoin).

**L2 (Layer 2):** A secondary framework or protocol built on top of an L1 blockchain to improve scalability and speed.

**Liquidity:** The ease with which an asset can be bought or sold in the market without affecting its price.

**Liquidation:** The process of selling collateral when a borrower fails to meet loan obligations or when collateral value falls below required thresholds.

### M-R

**Mainnet:** The live, production version of a blockchain network where real transactions occur with real value.

**Modular Blockchain:** A blockchain architecture that separates different functions (execution, settlement, data availability) into specialized layers.

**Oracle:** A service that provides external, real-world data to smart contracts on a blockchain.

**Private Credit:** Debt financing provided by non-bank lenders to companies, typically with higher yields than traditional bonds.

**RWA (Real World Asset):** Physical or traditional financial assets that exist outside of the blockchain but can be tokenized and represented on-chain.

**RWAfi (Real World Asset Finance):** The integration of tokenized real-world assets into decentralized finance protocols and applications.

### S-Z

**SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission):** The U.S. government agency responsible for regulating securities markets and protecting investors.

**Security Token:** A digital token that represents ownership in a real-world asset or company and is subject to securities regulations.

**Smart Contract:** Self-executing code on a blockchain that automatically enforces the terms of an agreement when conditions are met.

**Stablecoin:** A cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value by being pegged to a fiat currency or other stable asset.

**Staking:** Locking up cryptocurrency tokens to support network operations and earn rewards.

**TradFi (Traditional Finance):** Conventional financial institutions and systems, such as banks, stock exchanges, and investment firms.

**Tokenization:** The process of converting ownership rights in an asset into a digital token on a blockchain.

**TVL (Total Value Locked):** The total value of assets deposited in a DeFi protocol or blockchain network.

**Validator:** A network participant responsible for verifying transactions and maintaining the blockchain's security and consensus.

**Wallet:** A digital tool for storing, sending, and receiving cryptocurrencies and tokens.

**Yield:** The return on investment, typically expressed as an annual percentage (APY or APR).
